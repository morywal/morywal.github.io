Halloween Skeeball Tower Defense

My Role: Project Manager, Lead Fabricator, Designer
Date Created: October 2024
Context: "Game Design" Class, California Lutheran University
Skills: Project Management, Physical Fabrication, Unity, C#, Sensor Integration

The Project: A Physical/Digital Hybrid Game

The final project for our "Game Design" class was to create a Halloween-themed game for a local elementary school. We designed a physical/digital tower defense game based on a custom-built skeeball machine.

A critical design goal was to ensure the game was fun **with or without** the digital portion. This way, if any bugs or issues arose, the core physical game would still be a complete, fun experience for the kids.

  • Players roll physical balls up the skeeball ramp.
  • Sensors in the holes detect which lane the ball entered.
  • This sensor data is fed to a Unity game, which "shoots" a digital projectile down the corresponding lane.
  • Players must hit monsters in the lanes to collect "candy" (points) and prevent them from reaching the home base.

My Role as Project Manager: I stepped in to lead the project.

  • I led the physical fabrication, staying the final night **until 5 AM** to complete the assembly, often working by myself when others were unavailable. I particuarly enjoyed assembling sensors as I have worked extesnivly with arduino in the past.
  • We successfully transported and assembled the full game at the elementary school, tested it, and presented it to the students the next day.
