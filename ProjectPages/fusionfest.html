Fusion Fest

My Role: Co-Founder, Event Designer, & Project Manager
Date Created: November 2024
Context: Personal Initiative Project (CLU Community Class)
Skills: Google Slides, Designing and Pitching, Refinement based on Feedback. Presenting to a collection of all multimedia majors and minors at the school.

The Project: A Multimedia Jam

I co-founded and designed 'Fusion Fest,' a 48-hour multimedia jam, to foster a new culture of collaboration within the CLU Multimedia program. My role involved pitching the concept to faculty, designing the event structure, and mentoring student teams. The event was a major success and is now an annual tradition.

The Process: From Pitch to Event

The Challenge: I felt personally frustrated that we didn't have strong portoflios including more personal works. I also wanted projects that could help future students learn initiative for their future capstones. The multimedia program has many talented students but lacked a high-energy, collaborative event to bring them together and both build community as well as learn personal project development skills.

My Solution: I designed 'Fusion Fest' to solve this. My responsibilities included:

  • Writing the initial pitch and design document to get faculty buy-in.
  • Designing the theme, creative constraints, and judging criteria.
  • Structuring the 48-hour into an 1-2 week schedule to balance work-time with class time.
  • Managing event logistics and hands-off guidance.
  • Creating an event that could be easily recreated and used in the future after I leave.

The Impact: A New Tradition

The first Fusion Fest was a huge success, with over 60 students participating and 19 projects completed. Based on the positive feedback, the multimedia department adopted it as an Semester tradition, which was my ultimate goal and proudest achievement from this project.
