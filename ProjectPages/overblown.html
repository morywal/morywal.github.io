Overblown!

Overblown!

My Role: Project Manager, AI Programmer, Designer
Date Created: January 2025
Context: Global Game Jam 2025 (Theme: "Bubbles")
Skills: Unity, C#, AI Programming, GitHub Collaboration, Team Leadership

The Project: An Economic Bubble

The Prompt: For the 2025 Global Game Jam, our team was given the theme **"Bubbles."**

Our Solution: We interpreted this as **"economic bubbles."** In 48 hours, we designed "Overblown!," a quippy stock trading roguelike. We thought it would be fun to let players orchestrate a bubble for their own stock and take down competitors trying to do the same.

  • We created a stock game with multiple shops for Marketing, Research, Black Market, and Loans.
  • Each choice could benefit the player (raise stock value) or harm them (Black Market could hurt your reputation).
  • I also created a simple AI to play alongside the player.

My Role & Contributions

My job was to create the enemy AI, the general game loop, as well as the game's central win/loss condition. I also acted as Project Manager, helping to assemble all the visual and audio pieces into the final build.

  • I created a "GameManager" script that runs the game systems (turn order, win/loss checks).
  • Within the Game Manager I also implented the basic AI
  • I compiled all the external files from other group members (art, music) and integrated them into the final Unity project.

AI Brain Snippet (C#)

The core loop of the AI goes as follows:

  • Calls "ChooseAICardType()" as a random generator (as we did not have time to implement educated moves).
  • It then asks the card manager to check for cards.
  • It filters out all choices based on how much money it has, then picks a card at random.
  • If it's a "Black Market" card, it can choose an opponent to use it on.
  • Then it plays the card.

Impact & Reflection

This was my first ever Game Jam and I learned a lot. I had to take up a leadership role again, being the main form of communication among the team and doing a lot of the programming myself.

It was a challenge because I had to read and understand other people's code, but I was able to ask questions and work it out. I learned teamwork under a crunch.
